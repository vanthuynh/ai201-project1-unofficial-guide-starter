Los Liones Trail
Located in Topanga State Park, Los Liones Trail is a hike that’s certainly not for the
faint of heart, taking you on a steep climb that eventually takes you to the Parker
Mesa Overlook nearly 1,300 feet above sea level. While it’s certainly no easy task, Los

Liones Trail is perhaps the most rewarding trail on this list since once you make it to
the top, you’re instantly met with panoramic views overlooking the Pacific and
canyons around you.
If you’re looking for a rather challenging hike in the Westside, Los Liones Trail is your
best bet!

El Matador State Beach
Even if you’ve never done the hike at El Matador State Beach, it’s highly likely that
you’ve still had the opportunity to enjoy the scenic views that come with this
breathtaking trail. The steep hike that takes you down to the picturesque beach is
famous for being in The Notebook and Grease, and if you ever walk this trail, you’ll
quickly realize why movie location scouts chose it!
If Los Liones Trail is a bit too challenging for you and you’d rather have a leisure walk
with beautiful scenery, head to El Matador Beach—we promise you won’t be
disappointed!

Paseo Miramar
Located in our home of Pacific Palisades, the trail at Paseo Miramar is somewhat of
a hidden secret that we Palisades’ locals like to keep to ourselves since it’s just that
wonderful! Similar to Los Liones Trail, the Paseo Miramar path will take you up to
the top of the Parker Mesa Overlook for some awe-inspiring views of the coastline.
The 5.5-mile loop is popular for both leisure walkers and hikers looking to really feel
the burn. No matter what you’re hoping to get out of your hike, the trail at Paseo
Miramar will be nothing short of amazing.

Inspiration Point Trail
Inspiration point trail is the perfect trail for the entire family, with only a little over 2-
miles worth of hiking and little to no climbs! Located in Will Rogers State Park, the
trail takes you past Will Rogers’ impressive old ranch where you can even get a tour!
As for the hike itself, Inspiration Point Trail is all about having fun. If you want a
more challenging hike or perhaps a quiet walk to collect your thoughts, this is not
the trail for you since on any given day you can expect huge crowds!

Solstice Canyon Hiking Trail
Solstice Canyon is another one of those trails where no matter what time of the day
you go, you can expect to be accompanied by plenty of other people enjoying the
hike beside you and with good reason! Solstice Canyon is the perfect 3-mile long
loop for intermediate-level hikers that takes you along both scenic views, and even
an abandoned home is known as the Tropical Terrace Ruins, which was destroyed
by a fire in the early ’80s.
In addition to that, you’ll also walk past the Keller House, which is a historic hunting
cabin that will eventually take past a gorgeous waterfall, before heading up to the
most incredible views in all of Malibu at the top of Rising Sun Trail.
